    2. Graphs of Exponential and Logarithmic Functions.

    Sketch the graph of each equation below.
    1. \(\displaystyle y = 2^{-x}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle y = -2^{x}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle y = 2^{x}-1\)
    4. \(\displaystyle y = 2^{x-1}\)
    5. \(\displaystyle y = \log_2(x+1)\)
    6. \(\displaystyle y = \log_2(x)+1\)
    Hint.
    1. \(y = 2^{-x}\) is the graph of \(y=2^x\) reflected across the \(y\)-axis.
    2. \(y = -2^{x}\) is the graph of \(y=2^x\) reflected across the \(x\)-axis.
    3. \(y = 2^{x}-1\) is the graph of \(y=2^x\) translated down one unit.
    4. \(y = 2^{x-1}\) is the graph of \(y=2^x\) translated right one unit.
    5. \(y = \log_2(x-1)\) is the graph of \(y=\log_2(x)\) translated left one unit.
    6. \(y = \log_2(x)-1\) is the graph of \(y=\log_2(x)\) translated up one unit.

    3. Evaluating Logarithmic Functions.

    Evaluate each logarithm without using a calculator.
    1. \(\displaystyle \log_3(9)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \log_3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \log_{\frac{1}{3}}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \log_{\frac{1}{3}}\left(3\right)\)
    5. \(\displaystyle \log_{3}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt[3]{3}}\right)\)
    6. \(\displaystyle \log_{3}\left(1\right)\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle \log_3(9) = 2\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \log_3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = -1\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \log_{\frac{1}{3}}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = 1\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \log_{\frac{1}{3}}\left(3\right) = -1\)
    5. \(\displaystyle \log_{3}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt[3]{3}}\right)= - 1/3\)
    6. \(\displaystyle \log_{3}\left(1\right) = 0\)

    4. Evaluating Logarithmic Functions.

    Evaluate each natural logarithm without using a calculator.
    1. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\sqrt{e}\right)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\frac{1}{e^2}\right)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\left|-e\right|\right)\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(1\right)\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\sqrt{e}\right) = 1/2\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\frac{1}{e^2}\right) = -2\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\left|-e\right|\right) = 1\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(1\right) = 0\)

    5. Converting to Logarithmic Form.

    Rewrite each exponential equation in logarithmic form, but do not attempt to solve.
    1. \(\displaystyle 5^{x-1} = 6\)
    2. \(\displaystyle a^5 = x\)
    3. \(\displaystyle e^{x^2} = x\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{e^x} = \pi\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle 5^{x-1} = 6 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{\log_5(6)=x-1}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle a^5 = x \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{\log_a(x)=5}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle e^{x^2} = x \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{\ln(x) = x^2}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{e^x} = \pi \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{\ln(\pi) = - x}\)

    6. Converting to Exponential Form.

    Rewrite each logarithmic equation in exponential form, but do not attempt to solve.
    1. \(\displaystyle \log_4 (x+y) = y\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \log_{0.25}(\pi) = x\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \log_b(x) = z\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \ln(t)=0.5\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle \log_4 (x+y) = y \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{4^y = x+y}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \log_{0.25}(\pi) = x \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{(0.25)^x = \pi}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \log_b(x) = z \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{b^z = x}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \ln(t)=0.5 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \boxed{e^{0.5} = t}\)

    7. Properties of Logarithms.

    Given that \(\ln(x) = 8\text{,}\) \(\ln(y)= -1\text{,}\) and \(\ln(z)=3\text{,}\) use the properties of logarithms to find the value of the expresion.
    1. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(x y z^2\right)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\frac{x y}{z^2}\right)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\frac{x}{yz^2}\right)\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\sqrt[3]{x}\right)\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(x y z^2\right) = 8-1+6 = 13\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\frac{x y}{z^2}\right) = 8-1-6 = 1\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\frac{x}{yz^2}\right) = 8+1-6 = 3\)
    4. \(\displaystyle \ln\left(\sqrt[3]{x}\right) = \frac{8}{3}\)

    8. Properties of Logarithms.

    Express as a single logarithm.
    1. \(\displaystyle \ln(10)-\ln(5)+\frac{1}{2}\ln(9)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \ln(a)+\ln(b)-2\ln(c)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 2\ln(x+1)-\ln(x-1)-4\ln(x)\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle \ln(10)-\ln(5)+\frac{1}{2}\ln(9) = \ln(6)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \ln(a)+\ln(b)-2\ln(c) = \ln\left(\frac{ab}{c^2}\right) \)
    3. \(\displaystyle 2\ln(x+1)-\ln(x-1)-4\ln(x) = \ln\left(\frac{(x+1)^2}{(x-1)x^4}\right)\)

    9. Properties of Logarithms.

    Simplify each expression as much as possible.
    1. \(\displaystyle 2^{\log_2(5)}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2^{2\log_2(5)}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 8^{\log_2(5)}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 2^{2+\log_2(5)}\)
    5. \(\displaystyle e^{-\ln(5)}\)
    6. \(\displaystyle e^{2\ln(5)}\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle 2^{\log_2(5)} = 5\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2^{2\log_2(5)} = 25\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 8^{\log_2(5)} = 125\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 2^{2+\log_2(5)} = 4\cdot 5 = 20\)
    5. \(\displaystyle e^{-\ln(5)} = \frac{1}{5}\)
    6. \(\displaystyle e^{2\ln(5)} = 25\)

    10. Solving Exponential Equations.

    Solve each equation. Express your answer in terms of logarithms and use a calculator to obtain an approximation of the solution(s).
    1. \(\displaystyle 2^{t} = 10\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2^{t}-2 = 10\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 2^{t-2} = 10\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 2e^{t} = 10\)
    5. \(\displaystyle e^{-2t} = 10\)
    6. \(\displaystyle e^{t-2} = 10\)
    Answer.
    1. \(\displaystyle 2^{t} = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \log_2(10) = \frac{\ln(10)}{\ln(2)} \approx 3.32193\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2^{t}-2 = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \frac{\ln(12)}{\ln(2)} \approx 3.58496\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 2^{t-2} = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \frac{\ln(10)}{\ln(2)} + 2 \approx 5.32193\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 2e^{t} = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \ln(5) \approx 1.60944\)
    5. \(\displaystyle e^{-2t} = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = -\frac{\ln(10)}{2} \approx -1.15129\)
    6. \(\displaystyle e^{t-2} = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \ln(10)+2 \approx 4.30259\)

    11. Solving Exponential Equations.

    The voltage across a capicitor after \(t\) milliseconds is
    \begin{equation*} V(t) = 10 e^{-0.05 t} + 2. \end{equation*}
    1. Find the initial voltage.
    2. Describe the voltage after a long period of time.
    3. Find how long it takes for the voltage to be 5 volts.
    Answer.
    1. \(V(0)=10+2 = 12\) volts.
    2. The voltage will approach 2 volts.
    3. Solve \(V(t) = 5\) to find that \(t \approx 24.1\) ms.

    12. Avoiding Common Mistakes.

    Is \(\ln(A+B)\) the same as \(\ln(A)+\ln(B)\text{?}\) Is \(\left(\ln(A)\right)^n\) the same as \(n \ln(A)\text{?}\) Is \(\ln(A/B)\) the same as \(\frac{\ln(A)}{\ln(B)}\text{?}\)

    13. Properties of Logarithms.

    Prove that \(\ln(A/B) = \ln(A)-\ln(B)\) and that \(\ln(A^n) = n \ln(A)\text{.}\)
    Hint.
    Let \(x=\ln(A)\) and \(y = \ln(B)\text{.}\) Then rewrite each equation in exponential form.

    14. Properties of Logarithms.

    Prove the change of base formula for logarithms:
    \begin{equation*} \log_a(y) = \frac{\ln(y)}{\ln(a)}, \end{equation*}
    where \(a \gt 0, a\neq 1\) and \(y \gt 0\text{.}\)
    Hint.
    Solve the exponential equation \(a^x = y\) for \(x\) two different ways. Why must the solutions agree?
